Welcome from the Headmaster

We have seen some superb achievements this week from RGS pupils across a wide range of activities. The RGS Times includes some of these and it is exceptional that our Lower Sixth pupils have reached a national final for engineering, our debaters competed in an international final, one of our pupils is a National Judo Champion and our Hockey players are regional champions for the second successive year. To add to these, a Sixth Form pupil hit two centuries for the 1st XI in one week, one of our alumni fencers won an International Senior Women’s competition, and our Greenpower Team competed at Rockingham race track and won a trophy and medals for third place overall.
 
These performances, many of them at national level, are exceptional and come down to hard work and commitment from pupils and staff. Above all, they demonstrate that, if we can provide our young people with opportunities, give them confidence and encourage them to aim high, then the pupils seize these opportunities and demonstrate their considerable abilities. I am extremely proud of them all and many congratulations.
 
I encourage everyone to come along to the RHS Malvern Spring Festival next week (from 11 – 14 May) where the new RGS Worcester Gardening Club will have assembled a show garden in the schools’ section. We wish them all every success as they put the garden together this weekend.
 
Finally, please do encourage friends with young children to attend the RGS Springfield Open Morning tomorrow and the RGS The Grange Open Morning the following Saturday (both from 9.30am-12.30pm) and see all that goes on at our popular and excellent Prep Schools.
 
 John Pitt
Headmaster
